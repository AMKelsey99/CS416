FAQ

  • What do I need to bring to my first appointment?
  • Please bring any records of your companion animal from previous veterinarian clinics, adoption papers (if applicable), and your state ID. We may need you to fill in a questionnaire on contact info for future correspondence. The veterinarian will measure and record your companion animal's physical info and any medical conditions.


  • How can I request my pet's documentation for a new veterinarian?
  • At each appointment you should be provided with a detailed copy of your pet's health information. Please email or contact the front desk, and we will forward your pet's information to you.


  • What payment options does your clinic accept?
  • We currently accept VISA, MasterCard, Google Pay, Apple Pay, and PayPal. As of the start of 2021 we have gone cash-free.


  • Do you offer payment plans?
  • Generally, yes! Please contact the front desk to discuss details.


  • How do I refill my companion animal's prescription?
  • The veterinarian will write the prescription on an as-needed basis, and our talented support staff will reach out to you to schedule a time to pick it up.


  • I am having a medical emergency, may I call for assistance?
  • We do not provide medical advice over the phone. Please bring your pet to the nearest 24/7 emergency veterinarian clinic.


  • Does your clinic offer annual vaccinations?
  • Yes. If you would like reminders about your pet's vaccinations, please let the doctor know at your next appointment.
